Fried Rice

Fried rice has always been a staple of West African cuisine. Two main reasons for the continued popularity of this dish are its adaptability and, in most cases, the fact that it is mostly cooked to eat at one time with certain ingredients (green beans, pepper, etc.) are typical of those nutritious ingredients that make the cost of the meal worthwhile.

Jollof Rice

Everything from “everyday home kitchen” to celebrations, seasoned and simmered in flavorful tomato soup, is included. The classic version is cooked with long grain rice and seasoned with Nigerian curry powder and dry thyme. That’s it, with fried ripe plantain called dodo or coleslaw. The world may consider this a side dish, but keep in mind that it is the main dish in Nigeria.
